One missing tooth — and a choice: **single tooth implant or a bridge?** Both fill the gap, but they differ fundamentally, especially in what happens to the neighbouring teeth and the bone. Here's the honest comparison.

## The two options in brief

- **Implant:** a titanium root in the jaw + a crown. Stands alone, doesn't involve the neighbouring teeth.
- **Bridge:** crowns on two ground-down neighbouring teeth, with a "floating" tooth in between.

## Comparison

| Criterion | Implant | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Neighbouring teeth | **untouched** | must be **ground down** |
| Bone under the gap | **preserved** (implant loads it) | **shrinks** (no stimulus) |
| Durability | 20-25+ years | 8-12 years on average |
| Hygiene | like a natural tooth | harder under the pontic |
| Decay on supports | not relevant | risk under the crowns |
| Treatment time | longer (healing) | shorter |
| Upfront cost | higher | lower |

## The strongest argument: the neighbouring teeth

A bridge requires **grinding down two neighbouring teeth** — often perfectly healthy ones. That's irreversible. Those teeth then carry the load for three, and decay or a root problem can develop under the crowns later. The implant **leaves the neighbours alone** — its main advantage.

## The second argument: the bone

Under a missing tooth the **bone shrinks** because it isn't loaded. The implant transmits chewing forces and **stops the bone loss** — like a natural root. Under a bridge pontic the bone keeps receding.

## When a bridge can make sense

Honestly — a bridge isn't wrong in every situation:
- when the **neighbouring teeth need crowns anyway** (they're badly damaged)
- with **contraindications** to a surgical procedure
- when a **faster** solution is wanted

## Cost over the long term

A bridge is cheaper upfront, but its **shorter lifespan** and the risk of losing the support teeth mean future costs. The implant is a higher initial investment but usually **long-term**. [Cost overview →](/dental-treatment-cost-poland)

## Conclusion

For a single gap with healthy neighbouring teeth and good bone, the **implant is usually the treatment of choice** — it protects the neighbours and preserves the bone. A bridge is an option when the adjacent teeth need restoring anyway, or when an implant isn't possible. We decide after examination and a CBCT — at fair Polish prices.
