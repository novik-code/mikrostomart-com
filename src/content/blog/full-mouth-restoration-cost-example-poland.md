"How much do I **really** save — after travel costs?" The key question. Here's a concrete, realistic worked example for an extensive restoration in Poland vs the UK.

> Note: example figures for orientation. The real price is set only after examination and a treatment plan.

## Example case: patient, 58, upper-jaw restoration

**Situation:** several non-restorable upper teeth, wants fixed teeth.
**Solution:** All-on-4 in the upper jaw + two single crowns in the lower jaw + professional cleaning.

### Cost comparison

| Item | Poland | UK (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| All-on-4 upper jaw (4 implants + fixed bridge) | €7,500 | £15,000 |
| 2 single crowns (lower jaw) | €700 | £1,500 |
| CBCT + planning | included | £150 |
| Professional cleaning | €50 | £90 |
| **Treatment total** | **~€8,250** | **~£16,740** |

### Travel costs (2 trips, realistic)

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| 2× flights return (optional +1 person) | €150-450 |
| 2× accommodation (3-4 nights each) | €250-550 |
| Food, transfers | €150-300 |
| **Travel total** | **~€550-1,300** |

### Bottom line

| | Amount |
|---|---|
| Treatment in Poland | ~€8,250 |
| + travel | ~€1,000 |
| **Total Poland** | **~€9,250** |
| **Total UK** | **~£16,740 (≈ €19,500)** |
| **Saving** | **~€10,000 (≈ 50%+)** |

Even with travel costs counted generously, the **saving runs into five figures**.

## When does the trip pay off?

Rule of thumb: **from about €1,500-2,000 of treatment**, the saving clearly exceeds travel costs. For a single filling the trip isn't worth it — but for implants, crowns, bridges or a full restoration it clearly is.

## What affects the price

- **Scope** (single implant vs a whole arch)
- **Bone grafting** needed? ([sinus lift/augmentation](/dental-implants-poland))
- **Material choice** (implant brand, type of ceramic)
- **Number of trips** (often fewer with immediate loading)
