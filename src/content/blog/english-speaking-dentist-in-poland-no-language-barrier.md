One of the biggest worries before treatment abroad is: **"How will I communicate? Will I understand what's being done to me?"** For a medical procedure, that's a completely fair concern. At our clinic that barrier doesn't exist — treatment runs in English.

## Why language matters in medicine

For dental treatment you need to be able to:
- describe your **symptoms and history** accurately
- understand and agree to the **treatment plan**
- be clearly informed about **risks, alternatives and costs**
- understand **aftercare instructions** correctly

If that only works through gestures or a translation app, the very thing that matters — informed choice and trust — suffers.
