"Are implants made of metal — is there a metal-free option?" A common question, especially from patients who prefer metal-free solutions. There are indeed two material worlds: **titanium** (the decades-long standard) and **ceramic/zirconia** (the metal-free alternative). Here's the honest comparison.

## Titanium implants — the proven standard

Titanium has been the most widely used implant material for decades.
- **Extremely well documented** — long-term data over 20+ years, high survival rates.
- **Excellent osseointegration** — titanium reliably fuses with bone.
- **Mechanically very strong**, even in thin diameters.
- **Two-piece systems** (implant + abutment separate) — flexible for prosthetics, suited to complex cases like [All-on-4](/all-on-4-poland).

**Possible drawbacks:**
- with very thin gums, a **greyish shadow** can show through at the margin (relevant in the aesthetic front zone),
- true titanium allergies are **very rare**, but some patients cite this as a reason for going metal-free.

## Ceramic (zirconia) implants — the metal-free option

Zirconia is a high-performance ceramic.
- **Metal-free** — the key argument for patients who want it.
- **White/tooth-coloured** — no grey show-through, an **aesthetic advantage** with thin gums in the visible zone.
- **Highly biocompatible**, gums attach well, low plaque accumulation.

**Possible drawbacks:**
- **shorter long-term documentation** than titanium (fewer decades of data),
- often **one-piece** — less flexible for complex prosthetics and angle correction,
- greater material **brittleness** (fracture risk was historically a concern; modern zirconia is much improved),
- usually **more expensive**.

## At-a-glance comparison

| Criterion | Titanium | Ceramic (zirconia) |
|---|---|---|
| Long-term data | Extensive (20+ yrs) | Shorter |
| Aesthetics (thin gums) | Possible grey show-through | Excellent (tooth-coloured) |
| Metal-free | No | Yes |
| Mechanical strength | Very high | High, but more brittle |
| Prosthetic flexibility | High (two-piece) | Lower (often one-piece) |
| Complex cases (All-on-4) | Ideal | Limited |
| Cost | Lower | Higher |

## Which material for whom?

- **Titanium:** the default recommendation for most cases — best evidence, flexible, ideal for [All-on-4 / All-on-6](/all-on-4-poland) and complex restorations.
- **Ceramic (zirconia):** makes sense for an **explicit wish for metal-free** treatment and for an **aesthetically critical front zone with thin gums** — provided the case is suitable.

## What matters more than the material

Honestly: with both materials, **diagnostics (CBCT), planning, surgical technique and your oral hygiene** decide success more than the material choice alone. A well-planned titanium implant in good hands beats any "premium material" without proper planning.

## Conclusion

**Titanium** is the proven all-rounder with the best evidence and greatest flexibility — the right choice for most patients. **Ceramic (zirconia)** is the metal-free, highly aesthetic alternative for selected cases, with somewhat shorter documentation and higher cost.
